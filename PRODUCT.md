# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

ClassVybes serves mobile-first learners who need revision to fit around real life. The primary audience is secondary-school, university, polytechnic, college, and professional-certification students. Many study on phones, work with inconsistent connectivity, and already use music throughout the day. The secondary audience is teachers, lecturers, tutors, parents, and educational institutions evaluating whether the experience is accurate, safe, and useful.

The learner's core job is to turn dense notes into a form they will willingly replay and can recall later. The educator's core job is to understand how the platform supports learning without hiding errors or weakening academic rigor.

## Product Purpose

ClassVybes converts uploaded study material into educational songs across genres such as Afrobeats, Hip-Hop, Amapiano, Pop, Gospel, Classical, Jazz, and Lo-Fi. It analyzes the material, identifies key concepts, creates reviewable lyrics, generates a track, and supports repeated listening through lyrics, looping, playlists, and progress tools.

The landing page must make the notes-to-song transformation understandable within five seconds, earn trust around accuracy and privacy, and move interested learners toward early access. Success means visitors can answer three questions immediately: what ClassVybes does, whether it is for them, and what to do next.

## Brand Personality

Rhythmic, sharp, and encouraging.

The voice is culturally alive without becoming a costume. It is smart enough for educators, direct enough for a student between classes, and energetic without using forced slang. ClassVybes should make revision feel inviting while treating learning outcomes and user trust seriously.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI tool marketing: purple glow, robot imagery, floating dashboard cards, and vague claims about intelligence.
- Template EdTech pages: three identical feature cards, stock classroom photography, childish primary colors, and institutional copy that talks past students.
- Music-app cosplay: neon nightclub visuals, decorative waveform overload, autoplaying audio, and illegible dark interfaces.
- Unsubstantiated proof: invented retention percentages, fake testimonials, fake university logos, fake user counts, or unsupported compliance badges.
- Interaction for spectacle: scroll-jacking, inaccessible carousels, motion without a reduced-motion path, or controls that imply a working audio product when none is connected.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the transformation.** Make the path from notes to a replayable study track tangible before listing features.
2. **Let the learner lead.** Frame the story around study pressure, daily context, and the relief of revision that travels with them.
3. **Energy with evidence.** Use music-native art direction and motion, but keep product claims modest, specific, and research-aware.
4. **Trust is part of the product.** Explain review, privacy, processing time, and AI limitations in plain language.
5. **Mobile is the primary canvas.** Core content, controls, and calls to action must work comfortably on a small phone and slower connection.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Use semantic landmarks, visible focus states, keyboard-operable navigation and carousels, touch targets of at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels, readable type at 200% zoom, and color combinations that do not carry meaning alone. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`, avoid autoplaying sound, provide descriptive alt text, and keep all essential information available without animation. Use language that welcomes different education levels and does not assume a specific school, device, or learning ability.
