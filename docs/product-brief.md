# ClassVybes Landing Page Brief

## Objective

Introduce ClassVybes as a new kind of revision tool, explain the notes-to-track workflow within five seconds, establish a credible trust posture, and convert interested learners into early-access signups.

## Primary audience

Mobile-first tertiary and professional-exam learners who already use music throughout the day and want revision to fit outside a traditional study session.

## Single-minded proposition

ClassVybes turns the key ideas in your study material into genre-based study tracks you can replay, recall, and check.

## Positioning

ClassVybes is not a general AI music generator and not an all-in-one study dashboard. It owns music-led recall. The product combines personalized genre choice, reviewable lyrics, source-aware learning, and repetition in a familiar music experience.

## Desired response

"I understand this immediately. I would use it for my next difficult topic, and I know what happens to my notes."

## Primary conversion

Join early access.

## Secondary conversion

Understand the workflow by moving through the transformation story and genre carousel.

## Message priorities

1. What it does: notes become study tracks.
2. Why it matters: revision becomes easier to repeat during daily life.
3. How it works: upload, choose a sound, review, replay.
4. Why to trust it: source review, privacy clarity, honest limitations.
5. What to do: join early access.

## Content boundaries

- Do not promise a specific retention improvement.
- Do not present passive listening as mastery.
- Do not invent testimonials, user counts, ratings, school logos, or compliance marks.
- Do not show interactive controls that imply a live backend or playable track.
- Do not address institutions before the student proposition is clear.
- Do not describe users as fixed auditory learners.

## Page architecture

1. Sticky navigation with one primary CTA.
2. Asymmetric hero with real student photography.
3. Notes-to-track story with three natural steps.
4. Manually controlled genre carousel using original cover art.
5. Study-anywhere image-led narrative.
6. Listen, recall, check, replay learning loop.
7. Feature composition for lyrics, loop, playlists, and progress.
8. Accuracy, privacy, and responsible-use section.
9. Early-access form, FAQ, and footer.

## Success measures

For launch testing, prioritize five-second comprehension, early-access completion, genre-carousel engagement, safety-section visits, keyboard completion, and mobile performance. Do not optimize for raw scroll depth.
