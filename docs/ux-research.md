# ClassVybes UX Research

## Research scope

This memo combines the July 2026 PRD, a visual review of direct competitors, current learning-science evidence, mobile-connectivity context, and AI-in-education safety guidance. It separates sourced findings from product hypotheses so the landing page does not present assumptions as proof.

## Core audience

The first landing page should speak directly to Nigerian university, polytechnic, college, and professional-exam learners. They already use music throughout the day, often study on phones, and need revision to work during commutes, campus walks, exercise, and chores.

Secondary-school learners, educators, parents, and institutions remain important, but should not compete for the hero message. Serving minors also requires additional consent, age-appropriate design, and safety work.

## Jobs to be done

- Turn dense notes into a repeatable study format.
- Review while away from a desk.
- Choose a genre that makes repetition feel natural.
- Confirm that a generated lyric has not changed an important fact.
- Save data, time, storage, and generation credits.
- Revisit difficult concepts through a listen, recall, check, replay loop.

## Evidence that shapes the product story

Musical mnemonics are promising, but effects vary by material, learner, melody, and study method. A systematic review found a beneficial result in many, but not all, studies. The landing page must therefore avoid fixed retention percentages and describe ClassVybes as another way to revise, not as guaranteed mastery.

Passive listening is not enough. Retrieval practice and spaced review have stronger evidence. The most credible product loop is:

1. Listen to a study track.
2. Recall or sing the idea without looking.
3. Check the lyric against the source.
4. Replay the track later.

The product should not claim to serve a special "auditory learning style." Evidence does not support matching teaching to fixed learning-style labels.

## Mobile and cultural context

Nigeria is strongly mobile-first, but connectivity is not uniform. The page and future product should use compressed media, explicit download controls, visible generation time, and resilient natural-flow layouts. Music choice should be central to the value proposition. Afrobeats, Amapiano, Gospel, Hip-Hop, R&B, and indigenous-language listening are active parts of the audience's music culture, not decorative labels.

## Competitive review

### Soundforge

Useful: the paste, choose, generate flow is immediately clear.

Weakness: the current page uses a centered dark hero, generic green accent, equal three-card process, and teacher-first framing. It looks interchangeable with many AI products and makes unsupported broad learning claims.

ClassVybes opportunity: lead with the student's daily context, make local genre fluency visible, and explain review and source accuracy.

### StudySong

Useful: concise transformation promise and familiar phone/player framing.

Weakness: six identical feature cards, generic blue UI, and third-party-looking social proof dominate the page. The page presents a category template instead of a distinct cultural point of view.

ClassVybes opportunity: use record-sleeve art direction, real student context, and a stronger learning loop instead of an all-in-one feature dump.

### NotebookLM

Useful: audio is grounded in user-provided sources, with clear limitations and a strong trust posture.

ClassVybes opportunity: map important lyric lines back to the source passage, support review before repeated study, and state whether uploads train any model.

### Spotify and Duolingo

Spotify is a useful reference for confident music hierarchy, cover-art systems, lyrics, and player familiarity. Duolingo is a useful reference for singular brand color, clear task language, and friendly motion. Neither should be copied. ClassVybes needs its own "annotated mixtape" system that combines record-sleeve energy with the clarity of a marked-up lecture note.

## Trust and safety requirements

- State how uploads are used, retained, and deleted.
- Keep generated tracks private by default.
- Label generated output and make it reviewable.
- Do not imply that a track replaces the original material.
- Avoid artist-name imitation and celebrity voice cloning.
- Do not promise copyright-free output without verified vendor rights.
- Add age-appropriate content safeguards and guardian consent before serving minors.
- Collect only data required for the immediate task.

## Landing-page implications

- Lead with "study tracks," not "AI music generator."
- Do not show a dead upload control or fake playable demo.
- Explain the transformation before listing features.
- Put the learning loop and trust posture before pricing.
- Use a manually controlled genre carousel with no autoplay.
- Use real student imagery and original cover art.
- Disclose the PRD target of 2-5 minutes as a product goal, not an achieved performance claim.
- Ask for early access only after the visitor understands the experience.

## Validation still required

Before publishing strong performance claims, run concept and usability sessions with tertiary students, secondary learners with guardian consent, and educators across at least two Nigerian institutions. Test five-second comprehension, trust after seeing the workflow, fears about uploading notes, genre authenticity, CTA clarity, and mobile-data expectations.
